FireCast LPC — Sources & Methods

FireCast LPC is a rapid situational awareness sketch tool for wildfire spread estimation. It is not a prediction — it is a planning aid for initial evacuation zone assessment.

Spread Model

Surface fire rate of spread uses the Rothermel (1972) model. Fire perimeter shape uses Anderson's (1983) wind-driven elliptical projection.

Rothermel, R.C. (1972). "A mathematical model for predicting fire spread in wildland fuels." USDA FS Research Paper INT-115.
Anderson, H.E. (1983). "Predicting wind-driven wild land fire size and shape." USDA FS Research Paper INT-305.

Terrain Multiplier

General slope-effect multipliers on head fire spread rate. The steep drainage 3× factor reflects chimney-effect acceleration documented in fire behavior research. These are general estimates, not calibrated to specific La Plata County drainages. Use the Custom multiplier to enter your own value based on local experience.

Fuel Models

Scott & Burgan (2005) FBFM40 system. Auto fuel assignment uses elevation-band approximations for SW Colorado vegetation zones.

Scott, J.H. & Burgan, R.E. (2005). "Standard fire behavior fuel models." USDA FS GTR RMRS-GTR-153.

Dead Fuel Moisture

1-hour equilibrium moisture content from Simard (1968) using current temp/RH. 10-hour and 100-hour use time-lag weighted response to recent weather per Nelson (2000). The vegetation condition selector adjusts all moisture values together to reflect seasonal conditions.

Simard, A.J. (1968). "The moisture content of forest fuels." FFRI Information Report FF-X-14.
Nelson, R.M. Jr. (2000). "Prediction of diurnal change in 10-h fuel stick moisture content." Can. J. For. Res. 30(7):1105-1116.

Live Fuel Moisture

Operator-selected based on current vegetation conditions. Ranges follow Jolly et al. (2005) seasonal profiles for western US montane vegetation.

Jolly, W.M. et al. (2005). "Season profiles of live woody vegetation moisture in the western US." Int. J. Wildland Fire 14:169-182.

Weather

Open-Meteo API (NOAA HRRR 3km / GFS blend). All values are operator-editable to match RAWS or spot forecasts.

Limitations

Surface fire only. No crown fire, no spotting, no diurnal wind shifts, no terrain-channeled wind fields. Uniform fuel, constant wind. Fuel from elevation bands, not LANDFIRE ground truth. Use for initial size-up and evacuation zone discussion only.
