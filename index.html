FireCast LPC — Sources & Methods

FireCast LPC is a rapid situational awareness sketch tool for wildfire spread estimation. It is not a prediction — it is a planning aid for initial evacuation zone assessment.

Spread Model

Surface fire rate of spread uses the Rothermel (1972) mathematical model. Fire perimeter shape uses Anderson's (1983) wind-driven elliptical projection with length-to-breadth ratio derived from effective midflame wind speed.

Rothermel, R.C. (1972). "A mathematical model for predicting fire spread in wildland fuels." USDA Forest Service Research Paper INT-115.
Anderson, H.E. (1983). "Predicting wind-driven wild land fire size and shape." USDA Forest Service Research Paper INT-305.

Terrain Modifier

The terrain multiplier approximates slope and channeling effects on head fire spread rate. The "steep drainage" 3× factor reflects documented chimney-effect acceleration in narrow drainages, consistent with observations from SW Colorado incidents including the Missionary Ridge (2002) and 416 (2018) fires.

Fuel Models

Fuel model parameters follow the Scott & Burgan (2005) 40 standard fire behavior fuel models (FBFM40). The auto fuel assignment uses elevation bands calibrated to La Plata County vegetation zones.

Scott, J.H. & Burgan, R.E. (2005). "Standard fire behavior fuel models." USDA Forest Service GTR RMRS-GTR-153.

Dead Fuel Moisture

Fine dead fuel moisture (1-hour timelag) is calculated from current temperature and relative humidity using the Simard (1968) equilibrium moisture content equations. 10-hour and 100-hour moisture uses time-lag weighted response to recent weather.

Simard, A.J. (1968). "The moisture content of forest fuels." Forest Fire Research Institute, Information Report FF-X-14.
Nelson, R.M. Jr. (2000). "Prediction of diurnal change in 10-h fuel stick moisture content." Can. J. For. Res. 30(7):1105-1116.

Live Fuel Moisture

Live herbaceous fuel moisture is selected by the operator based on current vegetation conditions. Ranges follow Jolly et al. (2005) seasonal profiles for western US montane vegetation.

Jolly, W.M. et al. (2005). "Season profiles of live woody vegetation moisture in the western US." Int. J. Wildland Fire 14:169-182.

Weather

Current conditions from Open-Meteo API (NOAA HRRR 3km / GFS blend). All values are operator-editable to match RAWS observations or spot forecasts.

Limitations

This tool models surface fire only. No crown fire initiation, no spotting, no diurnal wind shifts, no terrain-channeled wind fields. The elliptical spread assumes uniform fuel and constant wind. Fuel assignments from elevation bands are approximations. Use for initial size-up and evacuation zone discussion only.
