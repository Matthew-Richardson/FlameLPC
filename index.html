FireCast LPC — Sources & Methods

FireCast LPC is a rapid situational awareness sketch tool for wildfire spread estimation. It is not a prediction — it is a planning aid for initial evacuation zone assessment.

Spread Model

Surface fire rate of spread uses the Rothermel (1972) mathematical model. Fire perimeter shape uses Anderson's (1983) wind-driven elliptical projection with length-to-breadth ratio derived from wind speed.

Rothermel, R.C. (1972). "A mathematical model for predicting fire spread in wildland fuels." USDA Forest Service Research Paper INT-115.
Anderson, H.E. (1983). "Predicting wind-driven wild land fire size and shape." USDA Forest Service Research Paper INT-305.

Fuel Models

Fuel model parameters follow the Scott & Burgan (2005) 40 standard fire behavior fuel models (FBFM40 system). The embedded fuel zone map for La Plata County uses elevation-band assignments calibrated to regional vegetation: piñon-juniper (GS2), Gambel oak/mountain shrub (SH5), mixed conifer (TU5), spruce-fir (TL5), and subalpine meadow (GR2).

Scott, J.H. & Burgan, R.E. (2005). "Standard fire behavior fuel models." USDA Forest Service GTR RMRS-GTR-153.

Dead Fuel Moisture

1-hour fuel moisture is estimated from current temperature and relative humidity using the Simard (1968) equilibrium moisture content equations, which underlie the NFDRS fuel moisture calculations. 10-hour and 100-hour moisture content uses time-lag weighted response to recent weather history following Nelson (2000).

Simard, A.J. (1968). "The moisture content of forest fuels — A review of the basic concepts." Forest Fire Research Institute Information Report FF-X-14.
Nelson, R.M. Jr. (2000). "Prediction of diurnal change in 10-h fuel stick moisture content." Canadian Journal of Forest Research 30(7):1105-1116.

Live Fuel Moisture

Live herbaceous fuel moisture uses a seasonal approximation for SW Colorado montane vegetation, ranging from ~30% (late summer/drought) to ~120% (spring green-up), adjusted by recent precipitation and day of year.

Jolly, W.M. et al. (2005). "Season profiles of live woody vegetation moisture in the western US." International Journal of Wildland Fire 14:169-182.

Weather Data

Current conditions from Open-Meteo API using NOAA HRRR (3km) and GFS model blends. Temperature, humidity, wind, and precipitation history are used to derive fuel moisture values. All weather inputs are editable to match RAWS observations or spot forecasts.

Limitations

This tool models surface fire only — no crown fire initiation, no spotting, no diurnal wind shifts, no terrain-channeled winds. Fuel assignments are approximations from elevation bands, not LANDFIRE ground truth. The elliptical spread assumes uniform fuel and constant wind. Use for initial size-up and evacuation zone discussion only.
